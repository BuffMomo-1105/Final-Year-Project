Potato - आलु

The potato is now Nepal's second staple food crop, after rice.Potatoes are widely grown in Nepal, at below 100 m altitude in the south to as high as 4 000 m in the northern mountains.

Apple - स्याऊ

There are districts such as Manang, Kalikot, Humla and others , especially in the mountainous north-west of Nepal, where apples grow. The harvest season in Jumla is from August to October. In some apple-producing districts, the harvest is from September to mid-December.

Millet - कोदो

Millet is the second most important crop of Nepal. A variety of millets, including Kodo (finger millet), Junelo (sorghum millet), Kaguno (foxtail millet), Chino (proso millet) are grown across different agro-climatic zones in Nepal. Baglung district of Gandaki Province has the largest area and production under finger millet in Nepal. The highest productivity of finger millet is found in Udaypur (1.97 mt/ha) followed by Panchthar (1.97 mt/ha) and Okhaldhunga districts (1.97 mt/ha)
